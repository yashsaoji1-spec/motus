Before you begin

What we collect

  • Hand and finger movement data via your device camera
  • Exercise performance (reps, range of motion, pain level)
  • Session history shared with your assigned therapist
  • Messages between you and your therapist

How we use it

  • Your data is only shared with your connected therapist
  • Camera video is processed on-device and never stored
  • Data is encrypted in transit and stored securely
  • You may request deletion of your data at any time
Please check the box to confirm your consent before continuing.

Questions? Contact your therapist or email support before proceeding.

Getting Started
Follow these steps with each new patient.
1
Give your patient your clinic code
Your code is displayed on the left under YOUR CLINIC CODE. Have your patient open phalanX, create a patient account, and type in that code. Their name will appear in your sidebar once they've connected.
2
Calibrate their hand — do this in person before the first session
Click the patient's name in the sidebar. At the top of their panel, press Calibrate Patient. The camera will open. Guide the patient through 3 poses one at a time:

Pose 1 — Extension: Patient holds their hand completely flat and straight.
Pose 2 — Mid-range: Patient bends all fingers to about halfway.
Pose 3 — Full flexion: Patient curls fingers in as far as they can go.

At each pose: press Capture 3s Sample, wait 3 seconds, then type your goniometer reading for each joint into the True ° column. Press Next Pose. After the third pose, press Save Calibration. Done — the app will now correct its own angle errors for this patient automatically.
3
Assign an exercise
In the patient panel, scroll down and click Add Exercise to Protocol to expand it. Choose an exercise type from the dropdown, set the number of reps, sets, and how often (e.g. daily). Press Save Protocol. The patient will immediately see a Start a Session button on their home screen.
4
Track their progress after each session
Click the patient's name any time to open their panel. You'll see:

ROM chart — range of motion over time
Pain chart — self-reported pain after each session
Joint Monitoring — click to expand, select which joints to track; peak angles are recorded every session
Session History — full log of every completed session
